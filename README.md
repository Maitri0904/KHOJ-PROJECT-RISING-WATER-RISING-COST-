<h1>Vadodara Flood Impact Analysis</h1>

<p><strong>Project Type:</strong> Data Analysis & Impact Assessment</p>
<p><strong>Focus:</strong> Financial, structural, and social impact of urban flooding</p>
<p><strong>Study Area:</strong> Vadodara City (Five most-affected areas)</p>

<hr>

<h2>üìå Project Overview</h2>
<p>
This project analyzes the financial and structural damage caused by recurring floods in Vadodara.
Using survey data from the five most-affected areas, the study quantifies economic losses,
housing damage, cleaning costs, and the disproportionate impact on different income groups.
</p>

<p>
The findings highlight that flooding is not an isolated event but a recurring disaster,
impacting the city approximately once every five years.
</p>

<hr>

<h2>üåä Flood Severity & Duration</h2>
<ul>
    <li>Average water level on roads reached <strong>4.99 feet</strong>, severely restricting transportation and emergency access.</li>
    <li>Floodwater remained inside homes for an average of <strong>59.18 hours</strong> (nearly 2.5 days).</li>
    <li>During the first 2‚Äì3 days, indoor water levels ranged between <strong>2 to 5 feet</strong>, creating dangerous living conditions.</li>
</ul>

<h2>üìä Key Insights</h2>
<ul>
    <li>Flooding in Vadodara is a recurring disaster occurring approximately every 5 years.</li>
    <li>Lower and Middle Income Groups face the highest financial stress due to housing vulnerability.</li>
    <li>Manjalpur consistently emerges as the most impacted area across all damage categories.</li>
    <li>Wall plaster and paint repairs dominate structural costs.</li>
    <li>Cleaning activities alone account for nearly one-third of total flood-related expenses.</li>
</ul>
